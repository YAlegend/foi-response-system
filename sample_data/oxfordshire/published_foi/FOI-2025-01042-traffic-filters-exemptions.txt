Request for information under the Freedom of Information Act 2000

Thank you for your request received on 16 April 2025, which has been considered under the Freedom of Information Act 2000.

I can confirm that Oxfordshire County Council does hold the information you have requested. Please see our response to each of your questions below.

You asked:

1. How many ANPR cameras will be used to enforce the proposed Oxford traffic filters?
   Our response: The scheme as consulted provides for six traffic filter locations, each enforced by automatic number plate recognition (ANPR) cameras. The filters operate by camera enforcement only; there are no physical road closures or barriers.

2. How many residents would be eligible for a permit to drive through the filters?
   Our response: Under the proposed permit scheme, residents of Oxford and a number of surrounding areas would be eligible to apply for a permit allowing them to drive through the filters on up to 100 days per year. Based on the eligible area defined at consultation, approximately 200,000 residents would fall within the eligible permit zone.

3. Which vehicles are exempt from the traffic filters?
   Our response: The proposed exemptions include blue badge holders, buses, coaches, taxis and private hire vehicles, emergency services, health and social care workers on duty, HGVs accessing premises, and vehicles registered to businesses in the filter area. Exemptions are applied automatically against the DVLA record or the relevant permit register.
