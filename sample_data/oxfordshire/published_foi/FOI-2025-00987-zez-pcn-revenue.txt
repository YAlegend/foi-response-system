Request for information under the Freedom of Information Act 2000

Thank you for your request received on 12 March 2025, which has been considered under the Freedom of Information Act 2000. The Oxford Zero Emission Zone pilot is operated jointly by Oxford City Council and Oxfordshire County Council; this response covers information held by the County Council as highway authority.

I can confirm that the council holds some of the information you have requested.

You asked:

1. What are the daily charges under the Zero Emission Zone pilot?
   Our response: During the pilot, vehicles entering the ZEZ pilot area during operating hours are charged according to their emissions. Zero emission vehicles pay nothing. The charges are banded: the lowest band applies to ultra-low emission vehicles and the highest band to the most polluting vehicles, with daily charges ranging from £2 to £10 depending on the vehicle's emissions standard. Charges apply between 7am and 7pm, seven days a week.

2. How many Penalty Charge Notices (PCNs) were issued under the ZEZ pilot in the last financial year?
   Our response: In the 2024/25 financial year, 18,640 PCNs were issued in relation to the ZEZ pilot area for non-payment of the daily charge.

3. How much net revenue was raised, and how is it used?
   Our response: Net income from charges and penalties under the pilot was £612,000 in 2024/25. Under the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984, any surplus from the scheme may only be applied to transport purposes, such as improvements to public transport, walking and cycling.
