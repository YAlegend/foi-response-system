Request for information under the Freedom of Information Act 2000

Thank you for your request received on 3 February 2025, which has been considered under the Freedom of Information Act 2000.

I can confirm that Oxfordshire County Council does hold the information you have requested.

You asked:

1. How many Low Traffic Neighbourhoods are currently operating in Oxford?
   Our response: As at 1 February 2025 there were three Low Traffic Neighbourhood schemes operating in east Oxford (Cowley, Church Cowley and Temple Cowley), made permanent following an experimental period.

2. How are the LTNs enforced and how many filters use cameras rather than bollards?
   Our response: The east Oxford LTNs are enforced by a combination of physical planters/bollards and ANPR camera-enforced filters. Of the traffic filters across the three schemes, seven are enforced by ANPR camera and the remainder by physical measures. Residents within the LTN zones may apply for a permit exemption at the camera-enforced filters.

3. How much penalty income was generated by the LTN camera filters in the last 12 months?
   Our response: Between 1 February 2024 and 31 January 2025, Penalty Charge Notices issued at the east Oxford LTN camera filters generated net income of £318,000 after collection costs. This income is ring-fenced for transport purposes under the Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984.
